## About Me
I am a PhD candidate at **Santa Clara University**, advised by **Dr. David C. Anastasiu**. My research lies at the intersection of **Generative AI** and **Physics-Informed Machine Learning**, with a primary focus on high-fidelity 3D MRI reconstruction and medical signal analysis.

## Research Pillars

### 3D MRI Reconstruction (AUSGAN)
I architected the **Attention-UNet Spectral GAN (AUSGAN)**, a physics-informed framework designed to accelerate MRI acquisition. By integrating a multi-scale spectral loss function, the model enforces consistency in the frequency domain (k-space), achieving a **17% improvement** in structural metrics (PSNR/SSIM) and a **20% enhancement** in tissue contrast over state-of-the-art baselines.

### Unsupervised Anomaly Detection (SUDO)
I am developing the **SUDO framework**, a multi-modal diffusion-based pipeline for the discovery of rare pathologies. By learning the manifold of normative anatomy from the **Human Connectome Project (HCP)**, the system identifies unannotated diseases as statistical deviations, providing clinicians with quantitative diagnostic heatmaps.

### Neuro-Physiological Signal Analysis
My work extends to the temporal domain, where I design deep learning classifiers to extract objective functional biomarkers from non-stationary **EEG signals**. This research aims to provide a quantitative foundation for psychiatric diagnoses, such as schizophrenia, traditionally reliant on subjective clinical observation.
